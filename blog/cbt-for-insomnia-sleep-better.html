CBT for Insomnia: Practical Ways to Sleep Better

January 18, 2025 · 6 min read

CBT-I (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia) is a highly effective, non‑drug approach to better sleep. It targets the thoughts, behaviors, and routines that keep you awake.

1. Stimulus Control

Reserve the bed for sleep only. If you can’t fall asleep within ~20 minutes, get up and do a relaxing activity under dim light, then return when sleepy.

2. Sleep Window

Set a consistent wake‑up time and restrict time in bed to match average sleep. Gradually expand as sleep efficiency improves.

3. Wind‑Down Routine

Start 60–90 minutes before bed: reduce screens, dim lights, and do calming tasks like stretching, journaling, or reading.

4. Challenge Nighttime Worry

Schedule a “worry time” earlier in the evening. Capture concerns on paper and write a compassionate, realistic response to each one.

5. Caffeine & Light

Stop caffeine 8 hours before bed. Get bright light exposure within 1 hour of waking to anchor your circadian rhythm.

Small, consistent changes compound. Pair these with gentle self‑talk: “My body knows how to sleep; I’m creating the conditions to let it happen.”
