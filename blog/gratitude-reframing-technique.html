Gratitude Reframing: A Simple Shift with Big Effects

January 20, 2025 · 4 min read

Gratitude reframing is the practice of acknowledging what is working—even amidst difficulty. It broadens attention and fuels motivation.

Try this nightly: Write 3 things you appreciated today and why they mattered. Link each item to a value (kindness, learning, connection).
Reframe example: “Today was chaotic, but I’m grateful I handled two tough conversations with care.”

Gratitude doesn’t deny pain. It adds context and choice.
